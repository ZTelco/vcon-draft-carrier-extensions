Executive Summary

Brief Description of the Product

The vCon standard is a formally adopted container to serve as the base unit of conversation data. This project serves to adapt RingPlan’s infrastructure design to embrace the standard and utilize it whenever dealing with conversational data.

Key Objectives and Expected Outcomes

The primary objective is to provide a central repository for vCon data for use within and without The Company and to integrate all disparate communications services with this repository. This service aims to:

  • Enhance user access to call content through a standardized interface.

  • Store associated documentation about the conversation such as transcriptions, metrics, and identity of the communicating parties.

High-Level Requirements

  • Authenticity: Provide a central source of truth attesting to conversation integrity.
  • Data Storage: Efficient storage of vCon data with ease of retrieval.
  • Second-Tier Analysis: Integration with advanced language models for further analysis, including transcription, summaries, and data querying.

Stakeholders and Target Audience

  • End Users: Individuals seeking to understand what data is being collected about them.
  • Data Analysts: Teams who will utilize conversational data for further analysis and intelligence gathering.
  • Technical Teams: Responsible for the maintenance and optimization of the platform.

Purpose and Scope

Purpose of the Document

This document is intended to align all stakeholders on the functionalities, objectives, and operational guidelines of the vCon Standardization Project. It serves as a comprehensive guide for understanding the scope of the service, its capabilities, limitations, and the framework within which it operates.

Scope of the Product

The vCon Standardization Project is designed to:

  • Warehouse conversational data and make it easily assessable

  • Store ancillary data alongside the conversation in a central repository

  • Provide a basis for further analysis through second-tier language model processing, and for use by 3rd party data analysts.

Out of Scope Features

  • Creation of vCon data for conversations occurring outside of our network.

  • Adapting Legacy Systems to be used with vCon.

  • Integration with non standards compliant vCon objects.

Assumptions and Constraints

  • Conversations occour within our network on our current generation software platform

  • Adequate storage and processing capacity for handling the volume of conversational data.

  • Compliance with data privacy and protection regulations in handling and storing conversational data.

Product Overview

Product Vision and Objectives

The vision of the vCon Implementation Project is to revolutionize call center operations by leveraging a standardized container format for conversational data. The service aims to:

  • Warehouse Data: Provide a central repository for RingPlan and its Customers to store their conversational data

  • Second-Layer LLM Analysis: Process Conversational Data with advanced Language Models to extract meaningful insights.

  • Enhance Operational Efficiency: Provide call center agents with quick access to call summaries and historical interactions, enabling them to build rapport and address customer needs more effectively.

  • Increase Consumer Security: Provide a standardized GDPR compliant interface for end users to understand what data is being collected and how its used.

User Needs and Product Value Proposition

The service addresses several key needs in the call center environment:

  • Efficiency: Agents receive concise summaries and background information, reducing call handling time.

  • Insight: Automated analysis identifies key topics, sentiment, and actionable items from each conversation.

  • Customer Experience: Understanding previous interactions allows for more personalized and effective customer service.

  • Compliance: By utilizing a central repository customer data is guaranteed to be handled in a GDPR complaint manner.

User Personas and Scenarios

  • Call Center Agents: Use the service to quickly understand customer history and current needs.

  • Call Center Managers: Analyze call data for trends, training opportunities, and operational improvements.

  • Data Analysts: Leverage the transcription and LLM outputs for deeper business intelligence.

Market Assessment and Opportunities

  • Growing Demand for AI in Customer Service: The market is increasingly recognizing the value of AI in enhancing customer interaction and operational efficiency.

  • Opportunity in Call Center Optimization: There is significant potential in using AI for conversation analysis, both for improving customer experience and for streamlining internal processes.

  • Competitive Advantage: Offering advanced analysis like sentiment analysis and task extraction can set the service apart in a technology-driven market.

Detailed Requirements

Functional Requirements

Data Import/Export and Accessibility:

  • Ability to upload and download packaged vCon objects.
  • Options for syncing vCon data between services.

Searchable Data Storage:

  • Standardized query format for location conversations regardless of medium.
  • Traceable version history showing all changes, additions, and modifications.

Data Pipeline

  • Implementation of versioning notifications so that services can be made aware of when a vCon is updated.
  • Automated processing of vCon data based on customer supplied business logic.

Data Optimization

  • Embedded data should be offloaded to an object storage reducing vCon size
  • Data packing/unpacking should only happen at the service edge.

Non-Functional Requirements

  • Performance: Ensure high performance in a speed of retrieval.
  • Reliability: Robust and consistent service performance with minimal downtime.
  • Security: Strong data security measures to protect sensitive information.

Data Requirements

  • Data Handling: Efficient handling and processing of large volumes of conversational data.
  • Data Storage: Secure and scalable storage solutions for conversations and related data.
  • Data Analysis: Advanced capabilities for analyzing data using the second-layer LLM.

User Interface and Design Requirements

  • Intuitive User Interface: For searching conversations, downloading data, and setting up event subscriptions.
  • Responsive Design: Compatible with various devices and platforms used by stakeholders.

Hardware and Software Requirements

  • Server Specifications: Adequate to handle the processing and storage requirements.
  • Software Compatibility: Ensure compatibility with existing systems and potential integration with third-party applications.

Regulatory and Compliance Requirements

  • Data Privacy: Compliant with relevant data protection and privacy laws.
  • Recording Laws: Adherence to regulations regarding call recording and consent.

Event-Triggered Functionalities

  • Custom Triggers: Ability to create custom triggers for specific words or phrases (e.g., “pizza”).
  • Automated Alerts: Notifications or webhooks when specified trigger conditions are met, like the mention of certain words or phrases.
  • LLM Interpretation and Action: The LLM to interpret instructions and decide if a transcription meets the trigger criteria, subsequently initiating the appropriate webhook or WebSocket event.

User Flow and Design

User Journey Maps

Creation Phase

  • vCon data is collected as a result of conversations occurring on the platform. This data is encapsulated and sent to the vCon Repository

Storage Phase

  • the vCon is written to the central repository where its embedded data is stripped off. It will remain here and receive version updates from future processes.

Enrichment Phase

  • vCons are analyzed against customer supplied databases and the contact information about the participants integrated into the vCon

Enhancement Phase

  • Various ML models are used to extract meaningful data from the conversation.

Quality Measures

Quality Assurance Plan

The Quality Assurance (QA) Plan for the vCon Standardization Project will focus on ensuring the reliability, performance, and security of the system. The QA team, in collaboration with development and testing teams, will implement rigorous testing procedures at each release stage.

Acceptance Criteria

Acceptance criteria will be established for each release, outlining the specific functionalities, performance benchmarks, and security measures that must be met for the release to be considered successful. These criteria will be jointly defined by stakeholders, including technical teams, product visionary, and end-users.

Testing and Validation Procedures

  1. Unit Testing: Developers will conduct unit tests to ensure the correctness of individual components.
  2. Integration Testing: The integration of system components will be thoroughly tested to identify and address any issues in the interaction between modules.
  3. Performance Testing: The system’s speed of retrieval, data processing, and storage efficiency will be assessed to meet performance requirements.
  4. Security Testing: Robust security measures will be tested, including data protection, access controls, and compliance with privacy laws.
  5. User Acceptance Testing (UAT): End-users will participate in UAT to validate that the system meets their needs and expectations.

Risks and Mitigations

Risk Identification

  1. Data Privacy Concerns: The handling of conversational data may raise privacy concerns among end-users.
  2. Integration Challenges: Integrating advanced language models and third-party services may pose compatibility issues.
  3. Performance Bottlenecks: High volumes of conversational data may result in performance bottlenecks.

Impact Analysis

  1. Data Privacy Concerns: Could lead to a loss of user trust and legal consequences.
  2. Integration Challenges: Delays in project timelines and potential functionality gaps.
  3. Performance Bottlenecks: Degraded system performance, affecting user experience and efficiency.

Mitigation Strategies

  1. Data Privacy Concerns: Implement strict compliance with data protection regulations and transparent communication about data handling practices.
  2. Integration Challenges: Establish clear communication channels with third-party services, conduct thorough compatibility testing, and have contingency plans for potential delays.
  3. Performance Bottlenecks: Regularly conduct load testing and optimize system architecture to handle increasing volumes of data.
